Common Questions

What nutrition directors ask us

Do I need to run a bid to purchase from WFHC?
Probably not. All five starter items qualify for informal purchasing. Most Wisconsin districts can purchase up to $10,000 per transaction without a formal bid — and DPI-approved thresholds can go up to $50,000. Check your district's micro-purchase policy. These products are priced to make a pilot easy.
What if you run out of something I ordered?
We notify you at least 48 hours before your scheduled delivery if there's a supply issue. We offer alternatives from our catalog at the same or lower price. You approve in writing before we ship anything. We never send a substitution without your explicit authorization — ever.
What food safety documentation do you provide?
Every case ships with PTI-aligned lot coding, farm name and USDA Establishment # on the label. Our warehouse is HACCP/GHP/Harmonized GAP certified. Farm partners are GAP-certified and audited. We have a food safety manager on staff and a 4-hour recall response commitment. Full traceability documentation available on request.
Can I use this for my Farm to School reporting?
Yes. We provide origin documentation for every case. All products are grown by named Wisconsin cooperative member farms. WFHC is listed by Wisconsin DPI as a food hub aggregator. As of July 1, 2024, "local" may be used as a procurement specification for products like ours under USDA rules.
What does delivery look like for my district?
We operate refrigerated routes out of our Waupaca warehouse. You'll receive weekly availability, place your order by a set cutoff, and we confirm your delivery day and window. Receiving documentation is provided with every delivery. Contact us to confirm route coverage for your district.
Can I get spec sheets for our purchasing files?
Yes — complete one-page spec sheets for every SKU are available for download in the Resources section below. Each sheet includes item code, case pack, unit size, ingredients, allergen declaration, shelf life, storage requirements, substitution policy, and traceability/recall information.
